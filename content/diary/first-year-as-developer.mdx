## A Year Already?

It feels like just yesterday I was nervously walking into my first standup meeting, wondering if I belonged. Now, 365 days later, I can confidently say: I do.

This year has been a whirlwind of learning, building, and growing. Not just as a developer, but as a person who collaborates, communicates, and solves problems under pressure.

## What Surprised Me

The biggest surprise? **How much of software engineering is not about code.** It's about understanding requirements, asking the right questions, and knowing when to push back on scope creep.

I spent more time in meetings and on Slack than I expected. And honestly? That's fine. Good communication prevents more bugs than any test suite.

## Hard Lessons

- **Don't be afraid to ask questions.** The senior devs are happy to help. They were juniors once too.
- **Code reviews are gifts**, not criticism. Every comment taught me something.
- **Imposter syndrome fades** — but it never fully disappears. You just learn to work alongside it.

## What I'm Most Proud Of

Leading the CRM feature at FPT Software with a team of 10 was terrifying and thrilling. Shipping it on time — with 95% test coverage — was the highlight of my year.

## Looking Forward

Year two starts now. I want to dive deeper into system design, contribute to open source, and maybe mentor someone who's just starting out. The cycle continues.

> Growth isn't linear. Some weeks you feel unstoppable, others you feel lost. Both are part of the journey.
